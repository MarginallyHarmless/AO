Artistover

A logo for an Airbnb-for-musicians app — book artists, vocalists, DJs and bands for any kind of event. Visual hybrid between Airbnb and Luma, leaning toward Luma. Dark, refined, a touch tech, a touch warm.

§ 01

The Brief

An app called Artistover.

It started life as Band Over — that name was taken — and reborn as Artistover. The product is a marketplace: someone needs music for an event, someone else can play it, the app puts them together. Think Airbnb’s flow, with Luma’s mood.

§ 03

Visual direction

Dark, but not black.

Phase one is dark-only. The logo must work on dark; we’ll deliver an inverted variant for a future light theme. The site background isn’t pitch black — it carries a tint (blue, mauve, deep azalea, or a gradient — TBD on our side).

Reference moods: Luma for the overall posture, Stripe and frame.io for the gradient-over-dark treatment of small symbols and numerals — “make it pop, make it flash.”

Type specimen.

§ 04

Two directions

One Airbnb-shaped,
one Luma-shaped.

Present both. Spend the lion’s share of the time on the Airbnb-shaped route — the client wasn’t convinced by the Luma-shaped one and we shouldn’t over-invest there.

Direction A

Symbol & wordmark

Reference: Airbnb’s “bélo”

A larger mark to the left of the wordmark. The mark itself should mean something we can justify — not a shrug-shape. Possibilities to explore: an abstracted location pin, a music symbol, a fusion of the two, or an invented glyph that earns its keep.

Priority:   primary route
Direction B

Wordmark & tiny glyph

Reference: Luma

The wordmark leads, with a small symbol set alongside (Luma’s mark reads almost like an AI icon). Lower investment — the client wasn’t sold on this direction. Show one polished pass.

Priority:   secondary route

The R-to-clef idea.

Try this: write the wordmark, then take the leg of the R and redraw it as a long S-curve that abstracts a treble clef, sweeping forward and resolving at the end of the word.

Tonally, look at the Floria logo — but the variant without the hat. Lyrical, single-stroke, confident. We’ll brainstorm this on a canvas together and iterate from there.
